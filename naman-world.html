About
Origin Story

Born in Indore — a city that punches above its weight in business culture, street food, and opinions. Somewhere along the way I started reading case studies for fun, ended up researching dark stores and consumer behaviour, and then decided that moving to Ireland sounded perfectly reasonable.

I turned down a QS Top 6 MiM program to go to UCD Smurfit instead. People asked why. I said the uncertainty is the point. I spent the last year founding an entrepreneurship cell, interning at a freight company, doing cross-border ops at a startup, and learning that "wearing multiple hats" is startup speak for "we forgot to hire."

I study why brands succeed, why systems fail, and why Zara never runs a sale. I overthink things professionally. I occasionally build things that fail respectfully.

The GMAT Real Talk

I spent months preparing. Made colour-coded notes, joined study groups, bought the official prep pack. The GMAT had other plans. I failed. Not dramatically — just quietly, in a test centre in Indore, on a Tuesday. Then I studied more and applied to UCD with the DET instead. The path changed. The destination didn't.

Self Doubt Honest Notes

There are days I wonder if I'm building the right things, in the right order, for the right reasons. Most ambitious people feel this — they just don't say it in public because LinkedIn is a highlight reel. I'm choosing to put it here because I think honesty is underrated as a professional asset. Uncertainty is not weakness. It's just accurate information about a complex situation.

India → Ireland The Journey

Moving countries alone at 21 for a postgrad program is one of those decisions that sounds brave in retrospect and terrifying in the present. Student loan? Real. Culture shock? Incoming. Distance from home? Significant. Worth it? Ask me in two years. I chose uncertainty over comfort. The frontal lobe enjoys the chaos.

Personal Philosophy Things I Believe
Ambition is useful — but only when combined with patience and some tolerance for being wrong.
Curiosity compounds. The person who asks "but why did that actually work?" ten years in a row knows something others don't.
Most people quit too early. Not because things get hard — but because they expected it to feel different when it was working.
Nobody actually knows what they're doing. The confidence gap between looking like you have a plan and having a plan is larger than most people admit.
Strategy is just empathy at scale. Understanding why people do what they do is the only durable competitive advantage.
